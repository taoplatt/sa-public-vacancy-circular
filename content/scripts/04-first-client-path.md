# Series 04: the first client path (FC)

Six scripts that walk the actual path to a first paying client. Every script is
framed as instruction ("here is the exact message"), never as a case study of
an invented person. We do not say "Sarah landed three clients this way".
Nobody named Sarah exists, so she does not appear. When real waitlist members
share real wins later, that becomes its own (true) content.

Post this series in week three onward, once the account has given before it
asks (see `../calendar.md`).

## FC-01: the warm circles message

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): The message that gets your first brief

**Length:** 35s

**VO script**
> Here is the exact message. Send it to five people who already like you.
> Quote.
> I am taking on two small design projects this month, to build my portfolio.
> One room. A proper moodboard and shopping list. For a reduced fee.
> Do you know anyone whose place needs a rethink?
> End quote.
> Notice what it does.
> It asks for a referral, not a favour. That removes the awkwardness.
> It names the deliverable, so nobody wonders what they would get.
> And it sets a limit of two. Which is simply true. You are new.
> Send it to five people today. Word for word is fine.
> The full outreach pack is going in the kit. Waitlist in bio.

**Visuals**
1. 0.0-1.5s: hook card, "first brief" in terracotta.
2. 1.5-13s: screen recording of the message being typed into a real messaging app compose window (type it into your own notes or a chat to yourself, screen record it). The message text is the star; keep it legible.
3. 13-26s: text cards, one per beat: "Asks for a referral, not a favour", "Names the deliverable", "Limit of two (true, you are new)".
4. 26-31s: text card: "Send to five people today."
5. 31-35s: closing card: "Waitlist in bio."

**Caption**
Copy this word for word if you like. The first brief almost never comes from strangers. #interiordesign #sidehustle #edesign #firstclient

## FC-02: the free room trade

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Design one room free. On purpose.

**Length:** 35s

**VO script**
> Design one room free. On purpose. Once.
> Not for exposure. For assets.
> Here is the trade, and you say it out loud.
> You design a friend's room for nothing.
> In return you get four things.
> Before and after photos you may use.
> A short piece of written feedback, in their words, that you may quote.
> Permission to show the moodboard in your portfolio.
> And one introduction to someone else who might pay.
> Agree all of that before you start. Then deliver like they paid full price.
> One free room, on clear terms. Then you charge.
> Free work with terms is strategy. Free work without terms is a habit.
> The agreement template is in the kit. Join the list in our bio.

## FC-03: the estate agent angle

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Estate agents need you. Really.

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> Estate agents and letting agents have a permanent problem.
> Rooms that need to look better, faster, and cheaper.
> Empty flats that feel cold in photos.
> Tired rentals between tenants.
> So here is the approach.
> Email or visit three local agents this week.
> Offer one fixed price service. A staging moodboard, or a room refresh day.
> Typical UK range for a refresh day, two hundred to four hundred and fifty pounds.
> Agents are the opposite of a one-off client.
> They always have another listing.
> One agent who trusts you can quietly fill your calendar.

## FC-04: the discovery call

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Your first client call, scripted

**Length:** 35s

**VO script**
> Your first client call, fully scripted.
> Fifteen minutes. Four questions.
> One. What is this room for, and who uses it?
> Two. What do you hate about it right now?
> Three. What is the budget for the room itself?
> Four. When would you like it done?
> Then you say this, and only this.
> Thanks, this is really doable. I will send a short proposal tomorrow, with a fixed fee.
> Do not quote a price live on the call.
> Hang up. Breathe. Price it calmly with the calculator.
> The call sheet is in the kit. Waitlist in bio.

**Visuals**
1. 0.0-1.5s: hook card, "scripted" in terracotta.
2. 1.5-6s: stock clip of a phone and notebook on a desk (Pexels: "phone notebook desk flatlay"). Overlay: "15 minutes. 4 questions."
3. 6-20s: text cards, one question per card, numbered 1 to 4, timed to the VO. Keep each under two lines.
4. 20-27s: text card with the closing line in quote marks: "Thanks, this is really doable. Proposal tomorrow, fixed fee."
5. 27-31s: text card: "Never quote live. Price it calmly after."
6. 31-35s: closing card: "Waitlist in bio."

**Caption**
Four questions and one closing line. That is the entire first client call. Save this one. #interiordesign #edesign #sidehustle #freelancetips

## FC-05: the first invoice moment

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Your first invoice takes ten minutes

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> The first invoice is the moment this becomes real.
> And it takes ten minutes. Here is all it needs.
> Your name and contact details.
> The client's name.
> One line describing the work. Single room e-design package.
> The fixed fee you agreed.
> Your bank details, and a due date. Seven days is normal.
> Send it as a PDF, the same day you deliver the work.
> And no apologising in the email.
> You did the work. The invoice is just the receipt of that fact.
> There is a ready-made invoice template in the kit. Bio link.

## FC-06: the whole path in thirty days

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): First paying client in 30 days

**Length:** 40s

**VO script**
> The whole path, in thirty days. Here is the shape of it.
> Week one. Pick your one package, and make two practice moodboards.
> Not ten. Two good ones.
> Week two. The free room trade. One room, for photos and feedback, on agreed terms.
> Week three. Send the warm message to five people who already like you.
> And contact three local letting agents.
> Week four. Take the discovery call. Send the proposal. Do the work. Send the invoice.
> No ads. No website. No degree.
> Thirty days is a target, not a guarantee. Some people take six weeks. That still counts.
> This exact roadmap, with every template, is the First Client Kit.
> Twelve pounds when it launches. The waitlist gets it first. Bio.
