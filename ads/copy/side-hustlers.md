# Ad copy: side-hustlers

**Ad set:** `side-hustlers` (£3.50/day, see `ads/meta-plan.md`)
**Angle:** the 9-5 stays, the evenings become a design income. The first
£250 brief happens while still employed. Low risk, small concrete win.
**Creative slugs (for `utm_content`):** `side-hustlers-v1-evenings`,
`side-hustlers-v2-permission`, `side-hustlers-s1-moodboard`.

**Policy notes (applies to every ad below):** no personal-attribute
callouts (nothing shaped like "are you stuck in a job"), no income
guarantees, no before/after earnings claims. Fee figures are framed as what
designers **typically charge** for a brief, never as promised earnings.

---

## Primary text

### Variant 1, short (~40 words)

Some people spend their evenings scrolling. Others spend them building
moodboards for fun. The First Client Kit turns the second habit into paid
work: templates, pricing guidance and a plain-English path to a first design
brief. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 2, medium (~80 words)

The day job stays. That is the whole point.

Residential and e-design work has no legal gatekeeping in the UK, which means
an evening-and-weekend design practice is allowed to start small: one room,
one brief, one invoice. A typical first e-design brief goes for £150 to £250.

The First Client Kit is the ready-made version of the paperwork: pricing
templates, a client brief form, a plain-English contract and a 30-day path
to a first paying client. £12 when it opens. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 3, long (~150 words, storytelling)

Here is how a first design brief usually happens.

Someone who has rearranged their own living room eleven times finally says
yes when a friend of a friend asks for help with theirs. They spend two
evenings on a moodboard and a shopping list. Then comes the awkward part:
what to charge, what to put in writing, what happens if the sofa arrives in
the wrong green.

That awkward part is why most people stop at "free favours for friends". It
is not a talent problem. It is a paperwork problem.

The First Client Kit is the paperwork, done: a pricing guide (typical first
briefs run £150 to £250), a client questionnaire, a plain-English agreement,
and a 30-day plan for finding that first client on purpose instead of by
accident. No degree required, and in the UK none is legally needed for this
work.

£12 when it opens. Join the waitlist and keep the day job.

## Creative briefs (3)

Faceless throughout: hands, rooms, screens, paper. No presenter, no invented
founder, no fake testimonials or fake numbers on screen.

### Brief 1, faceless video: "Evenings" (slug `side-hustlers-v1-evenings`)

- **Format:** 9:16 vertical, 20 to 30 seconds, captions burned in, native
  TikTok-style pacing. Sound from Meta's licensed library only.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "evenings" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the "what I do between 7pm and 10pm" framing). Once
  TikTok analytics exist, swap in whichever hook from that family has the
  best 3-second hold rate; the ad inherits the winner, it does not invent a
  new one.
- **First-frame text:** "The 9-5 stays. The evenings do this."
- **Beats:** (1) hands laying out paint swatches on a kitchen table at
  night, lamp light; (2) screen recording of a moodboard coming together;
  (3) close-up of the kit's real pricing page with overlay "a typical first
  brief: £150 to £250"; (4) printed client brief form being filled in;
  (5) end card in brand plaster/terracotta, Georgia serif: "Your first
  paying design client in 30 days. Join the waitlist."
- **Voice:** on-screen text only, or a calm VO reading the overlays. No hype
  reads.

### Brief 2, faceless video: "Permission" (slug `side-hustlers-v2-permission`)

- **Format:** 9:16, 15 to 20 seconds, text-led.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "permission fact" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the "nobody tells you this about interior design in
  the UK" framing).
- **First-frame text:** "No, you do not need a qualification to charge for
  e-design in the UK."
- **Beats:** (1) the fact, held for 2 seconds over a slow pan of a styled
  shelf; (2) "What you do need:" followed by three quick cuts, each with one
  overlay: "a way to price it", "a brief form clients take seriously", "a
  plain-English agreement"; (3) kit flat-lay with "all three, ready-made,
  £12 when it opens"; (4) end card: "Join the waitlist."
- **Note:** the opening fact is the brand's core reassurance line; keep it
  word for word.

### Brief 3, static image: moodboard flat-lay (slug `side-hustlers-s1-moodboard`)

- **Format:** 1:1 and 4:5 exports of the same layout.
- **Concept:** overhead flat-lay on plaster (#F1E9DE) background: fabric
  swatches, two paint chips (one sage #7E8D77), a printed room photo, a
  pencil, and the kit's client brief form partly visible. Natural shadows,
  editorial not clinical.
- **Text overlay:** Georgia serif, ink (#221F1C), sentence case, kept
  small (well under a fifth of the image): "Turn evenings into design
  income." Small terracotta (#C4602F) sub-line: "The First Client Kit,
  £12 at launch."
- **No** faces, no laptops-with-fake-dashboards, no money imagery.
