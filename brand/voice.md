# Maison Method — brand voice

## Who we are talking to

People in the UK and Europe who love interiors — they rearrange furniture for
fun, they have opinions about paint, friends already ask them for help — but
who believe "real" interior design is gated behind a degree, a studio job, or
rich clients. They are side-hustlers, career switchers and stay-at-home
parents. They do not lack taste; they lack permission and a path.

## The one feeling every piece of copy should create

"Oh — I could actually do this." Permission plus a plan.

## Voice principles

1. **Permission-giving, never preachy.** We tell the truth that the industry
   hides: residential and e-design work has no legal gatekeeping in most of
   the UK/EU. Say it plainly, cite it, move on.
2. **Warm and specific, never hypey.** No "6-FIGURE EMPIRE", no rocket emoji
   energy, no fake urgency. We sound like a knowledgeable friend who has done
   the paperwork for you. Concrete numbers beat superlatives: "your first
   £300 room refresh" not "unlimited income potential".
3. **British English, sentence case.** Colour, organise, licence. Headings in
   sentence case. No em dashes; use commas, full stops or brackets. No emoji
   in brand copy (allowed sparingly in TikTok captions where the platform
   expects them).
4. **Sell the first client, not the empire.** Every promise is scoped to the
   believable near-term win: the first brief, the first invoice, the first
   room. The audience distrusts empire-sellers; the first client is the
   moment their identity changes.
5. **Faceless but human.** The brand is the method, not a guru. Write "we"
   for the brand, "you" for the reader. Never invent a fake founder persona,
   fake testimonials or fake numbers; social proof is added only when real.

## Words we use

Method, kit, path, brief, client, room, refresh, moodboard, fee, invoice,
launch, ready-made, plain-English.

## Words we avoid

Empire, hustle (as a noun of praise), passive income, 6-figure, guru,
masterclass, secret, hack, unlock (metaphorically), game-changer.

## Copy patterns

- Headline pattern: concrete outcome + time or money anchor.
  "Your first paying design client in 30 days, without a degree."
- Reassurance pattern: name the fear, dissolve it with a fact.
  "No, you do not need a qualification to charge for e-design in the UK."
- CTA pattern: low-stakes verbs. "Join the list", "Get the kit", "Start with
  one room". Never "BUY NOW" or countdown timers.
