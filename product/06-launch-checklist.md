# The launch checklist

The whole 30 days on one page. Print it, stick it on the fridge or inside a cupboard door, and tick as you go. Each item is explained fully in the roadmap (file 02); this page is just the drumbeat.

## Before day 1

- [ ] Read Start here and The first client roadmap in full (about 40 minutes)
- [ ] Decide your weekly hours budget and block them in your calendar (5 to 8 hours a week is the assumption)
- [ ] Check the permission facts for your country if outside the UK (five minutes, file 01)

## Week 1: decide

- [ ] Pick one lane: e-design / room refresh / styling for sellers / colour consultation
- [ ] Write the offer sentence: who, outcome, deliverable, fixed fee, timeframe
- [ ] Write the included / not-included list (including revision rounds)
- [ ] Set the price using the pricing guide and pricing-calculator.csv
- [ ] Put the offer and price in one public place (Instagram bio or Canva one-pager)
- [ ] Say the price out loud to another human without discounting it
- [ ] Send the free-project message to one chosen friend (piece 2 starts now, their diary is the bottleneck)

## Week 2: build proof

- [ ] Redesign your own room, exactly to your offer's scope
- [ ] Photograph your room before and after (daylight, landscape, lens wiped)
- [ ] Write the 100-word story for your room (brief, constraints, three key decisions)
- [ ] Deliver the friend's room as if they had paid, questionnaire and all
- [ ] Photograph the friend's room before and after
- [ ] Get the friend's two-sentence review in writing on the day you finish
- [ ] Create one fictional concept, labelled "Concept project (self-initiated brief)"
- [ ] Publish all three pieces in one shareable place
- [ ] Send the portfolio link to one honest person for a sense check
- [ ] Build the Google Form questionnaire (10 minutes, questions are in file 04)

## Week 3: tell people

- [ ] Send the circle 1 announcement personally to at least 20 people
- [ ] Post the circle 1 announcement publicly once
- [ ] Find three local Facebook groups plus Nextdoor and read their posting rules
- [ ] Post the circle 2 script in all three groups, leading with your best before/after
- [ ] Reply to every group comment with genuinely useful mini-advice
- [ ] List ten local estate agents, letting agents and landlords
- [ ] Send the circle 3 email to all ten
- [ ] Put the 4-to-5-day follow-ups in your calendar, and send them when due
- [ ] Reply to every enquiry within 24 hours
- [ ] Log every conversation (name, date, what they said, next step)
- [ ] Nudge every "maybe" once after 7 to 10 days

## Week 4: close and deliver

- [ ] Offer two time slots and book the discovery call
- [ ] Hold the call: listen 10 minutes, present 5, next step 5
- [ ] Say the fee and then stay silent
- [ ] Send the one-page proposal the same day as the call
- [ ] On yes: send the letter of agreement and the 50% deposit invoice
- [ ] Send the questionnaire link at the same time
- [ ] Start work only when the deposit has arrived
- [ ] Deliver on or before the promised date, with a friendly walkthrough note
- [ ] Complete one revision round (one consolidated list, within scope)
- [ ] Invoice the balance and confirm when paid
- [ ] Send the testimonial request within 48 hours of their happy reply
- [ ] Ask for photo permission

## After delivery (the ten-minute debrief)

- [ ] Log real hours in pricing-calculator.csv and check your effective rate
- [ ] Note anything requested that was out of scope (future offer material)
- [ ] Add the testimonial to your portfolio, proposal template and one public post
- [ ] Send one message to one person the client mentioned
- [ ] Raise your price 10 to 20% for the next enquiry

## If week 3 was quiet

- [ ] Count actual sends: did 40+ people receive the offer and price?
- [ ] Test the offer sentence on two honest people, rewrite if they cannot repeat it back
- [ ] Reshoot your best before/after if it is not genuinely strong
- [ ] Only then reconsider the price, using the pricing guide, not fear
- [ ] Run week 3 again: new groups, ten more agent emails, one more announcement

One client. That is the whole page. Tick the last box and you are a designer with a client, an invoice and a testimonial, which is a different person from the one who started 30 days ago.
