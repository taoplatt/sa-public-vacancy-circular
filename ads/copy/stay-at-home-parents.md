# Ad copy: stay-at-home parents

**IMPORTANT: this persona is a CREATIVE VARIANT, not an ad set.** These ads
run **inside the `side-hustlers` ad set** (see `ads/meta-plan.md`, section 1:
at £100 a third ad set would starve all three of signal, and Meta's broad
delivery reaches this audience within side-hustlers anyway). Read their
results at the **ad level**, tagged by the slugs below.

**Angle:** design work that fits around school hours, done from home, using
taste they already have. The identity thread ("something that is yours") is
handled with respect: parenting is treated as the schedule the work fits
around, never as a problem the product fixes. Nothing patronising, nothing
"mumpreneur", no suggestion that anyone has been wasting their days.
**Creative slugs (for `utm_content`):** `side-hustlers-sahp-v1-schoolrun`,
`side-hustlers-sahp-v2-yours`, `side-hustlers-sahp-s1-moodboard`.

**Policy notes (applies to every ad below):** no personal-attribute
callouts (nothing shaped like "are you a stay-at-home mum"). The copy
describes the shape of the work (school hours, from home), never the
viewer's family status. No income guarantees, no before/after earnings
claims; fees framed as what designers **typically charge** only.

## Primary text

### Variant 1, short (~40 words)

Some work fits inside school hours and some never will. E-design does:
moodboards, sourcing lists and room plans, built from a kitchen table, on
hours you choose. The First Client Kit is the path to the first paying
brief. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 2, medium (~80 words)

E-design is one of the few kinds of client work that genuinely fits school
hours: the client never needs a meeting at 4pm, the moodboard does not care
when it gets made, and the whole job happens from home.

The taste is usually already there. What is missing is the plumbing: what to
charge (a typical first brief runs £150 to £250), what to send, what to put
in writing. The First Client Kit is that plumbing, ready-made, £12 at
launch. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 3, long (~150 words, storytelling)

There is a particular kind of afternoon where the house is finally quiet,
there are two clear hours before pickup, and the question arrives: what
could be built in this slot, on repeat?

Here is one honest answer. E-design, done remotely: a client fills in a
brief form, and the designer sends back a moodboard, a floor plan and a
shopping list. No site visits, no evening calls, no commute. In the UK it
needs no degree and no licence. A typical first brief goes for £150 to
£250, and the work happens on whichever hours are actually free.

For a lot of people the point is not only the fee. It is having one thing
on the calendar that is theirs: their name on the invoice, their eye on
the moodboard.

The First Client Kit is the 30-day path to that first brief: pricing guide,
client questionnaire, plain-English agreement, client-finding plan. £12
when it opens. Join the waitlist.

## Creative briefs (3)

Faceless throughout: hands, rooms, screens, paper. No presenter, no invented
founder, no fake testimonials or fake numbers on screen. Crucially for this
persona: no children in shot, no chaos-to-calm clichés, no laundry piles.
The visual world is the work, not the parenting.

### Brief 1, faceless video: "School hours" (slug `side-hustlers-sahp-v1-schoolrun`)

- **Format:** 9:16 vertical, 20 to 30 seconds, captions burned in, unhurried
  pacing. Sound from Meta's licensed library, warm and quiet.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "school hours" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the "work that fits 9.30 to 2.30" framing). Swap in
  the family's best 3-second hold performer once TikTok analytics exist.
- **First-frame text:** "Client work that fits between 9.30 and 2.30."
- **Beats:** (1) hook over morning light on a cleared kitchen table, a
  notebook and swatches being laid out; (2) overlay "the client never needs
  a 4pm meeting" over a screen recording of a moodboard build; (3) overlay
  "a typical first brief: £150 to £250" over the kit's real pricing page;
  (4) materials being packed neatly away, table clear again; (5) end card,
  plaster background, Georgia serif: "Your first paying design client in
  30 days. Join the waitlist."
- **Voice:** on-screen text or calm VO. The pack-away beat matters: it says
  the work respects the day's shape, without a word about parenting.

### Brief 2, faceless video: "Yours" (slug `side-hustlers-sahp-v2-yours`)

- **Format:** 9:16, 15 to 20 seconds, text-led.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "something that is yours" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the identity thread). This is the emotional variant;
  keep it understated or it tips into patronising.
- **First-frame text:** "One thing on the calendar that is entirely yours."
- **Beats:** (1) hook over a close-up of a name being written at the top of
  the kit's brief form; (2) three quick cuts with one overlay each: "your
  eye", "your hours", "your invoice"; (3) overlay "e-design, from home, no
  degree needed in the UK" over a finished moodboard; (4) end card: "The
  First Client Kit, £12 at launch. Join the waitlist."
- **Note:** never pair this hook with childcare imagery; the "yours" line
  only works if the frame stays on the craft.

### Brief 3, static image: moodboard flat-lay (slug `side-hustlers-sahp-s1-moodboard`)

- **Format:** 1:1 and 4:5 exports of the same layout.
- **Concept:** overhead flat-lay on cream (#FAF6EF): a morning-light
  moodboard in progress, fabric swatches, sage (#7E8D77) and terracotta
  (#C4602F) paint chips, a mug, a wall clock face reading 9.30 tucked at
  the frame edge as the only nod to the schedule.
- **Text overlay:** Georgia serif, ink (#221F1C), sentence case, small:
  "Design work in school hours." Terracotta sub-line: "The First Client
  Kit, £12 at launch."
- **No** faces, no children's items in frame, no money imagery.
