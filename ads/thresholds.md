# Decision thresholds

The rules, stated once, bluntly. Set them now, before the data arrives, so
that nobody moves the goalposts mid-probe. Everything here reads together
with `ads/meta-plan.md` (structure and spend) and applies to probe 1.

## Landing page conversion (cold Meta traffic)

Measured as Leads ÷ landing page views, from Meta traffic only (use the
UTM-tagged GoatCounter numbers, not blended organic).

- **8% or higher: the message works.** Keep spending to plan.
- **5% to 8%: acceptable for a first probe.** Let it run; note which ads
  drive the better-converting clicks.
- **Below 5%: pause the ads and fix the page copy first.** Cheap clicks
  into a page that does not convert is paying to learn nothing. Rewrite,
  re-test the form, then resume.

## Cost per lead (CPL)

- **£2.50 or less: healthy.** Continue, change nothing.
- **£2.50 to £4.00: watch.** Keep running, check daily, do not intervene.
- **Over £4.00 after £25 spend in an ad set: kill that ad set** and
  reallocate its budget to the survivor. No second chances inside probe 1;
  new creative waits for probe 2.

## Tripwire (£12 First Client Kit)

- **Take-rate of 3% of leads or higher: strong real-money signal.** People
  paying £12 on a thank-you page for a product from a brand they met four
  minutes ago is the best evidence this probe can produce.
- **Any sale at all is meaningful at this scale.** With 25 to 60 leads,
  one or two sales is a real signal; zero sales from under 40 leads is not
  yet evidence of anything.

## Overall gates, after ~4 weeks (organic + paid combined)

- **300+ waitlist signups, or 10+ kit sales: build the full product.**
- **100 to 300 signups: iterate.** The interest is real but the message or
  persona is off. Rework messaging using the winning hooks, then re-probe.
  Do not build the full product on this middle band.
- **Under 100 signups with the budget spent: kill or pivot.** The kind
  answer and the correct answer are the same one: this framing did not
  earn attention, and more budget will not fix that.

## What NOT to conclude from £100

- **No persona winner declarations.** Under roughly 50 leads per ad set,
  a CPL gap between side-hustlers and career-switchers is noise wearing a
  trend's clothes. The same goes double for the stay-at-home-parent
  creatives, which share an ad set and will have even smaller numbers.
  Persona reads from probe 1 are hints for designing probe 2, nothing more.
- **No CPL forecasts.** Learning-limited ad sets at £3.50/day say nothing
  about costs at ten times the budget.
- **No tripwire percentage maths.** At this lead volume the take-rate has
  no stable denominator. Count sales as events, not rates.
- **No "Meta does not work for us".** £100 tests one message on one page
  in one country. It can kill this message; it cannot kill the channel.
