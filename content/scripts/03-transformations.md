# Series 03: transformations (TR)

Six scripts built around visual transformation b-roll. This series carries the
aesthetic pull of the account; the VO's job in every script is to tie the
visual back to one idea: this is a learnable, sellable skill.

B-roll rule: use only royalty-free footage (Pexels, Pixabay, Coverr, Mixkit)
or your own screen recordings. Never lift another creator's before/after. For
"before/after" effects with stock footage, pair a plain or unstyled room clip
with a styled room clip in a similar palette; you are illustrating the idea,
not claiming the rooms are the same project. Your own Canva moodboards are the
one asset that is always authentically yours; lean on them.

## TR-01: the before and after

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Watch this room change its mind

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> Same room. Same furniture, mostly. Different decisions.
> The sofa turned to face the light.
> A rug to anchor the seating.
> Curtains hung higher, to lift the ceiling.
> Two lamps, instead of the big overhead light.
> This is not magic. And it is not luck.
> It is a set of learnable moves.
> In the UK, people typically pay one hundred and fifty to four hundred pounds for someone to make these calls.
> You could be on the other side of this.
> The path there is in our bio.

**Visuals**
1. 0.0-1.5s: hook card over a dim, flat-lit room clip (Pexels: "empty living room daylight"), "change its mind" in terracotta.
2. 1.5-6s: hold the plain room clip. Overlay: "Before: no decisions made".
3. 6-20s: cut to a styled living room clip in a similar palette (Pexels: "cosy styled living room lamp"). As the VO lists each move, pop one short text label at a time: "Sofa to the light", "Rug anchors", "Curtains higher", "Lamps, not big light". One label on screen at a time, bottom third.
4. 20-26s: slow zoom on the styled room. Overlay: "Learnable moves. Typical fee £150-£400."
5. 26-30s: closing card on plaster: "The path is in our bio."

**Caption**
Four decisions, one room. None of them need a degree, all of them are learnable. #interiordesign #beforeandafter #ukinteriors #homedecor

**CTA:** Spoken close: "The path there is in our bio." Closing card: "The path is in our bio."
