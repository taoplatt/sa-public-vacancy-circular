# Production workflow: 7 faceless videos in ~90 minutes a week

One person, one phone or laptop, no filming, no face, no excuses. This is the
repeatable weekly batch. Run it the same day each week (Saturday morning works
well; the calendar's week starts Monday).

## The 90-minute batch

| Step | Time | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Pick | 5 min | Open `calendar.md`, list the 7 scripts for the coming week. No swapping mid-batch. |
| 2. Gather b-roll | 20 min | For each script, download the clips its Visuals section names (sources below). Save into one folder per script: `PM-01/`, `TR-03/`, etc. 2-4 clips per video is enough. |
| 3. Record screens | 15 min | Knock out all screen recordings for the week in one sitting: Canva moodboard builds, template scrolls, the typed outreach message. Phone: built-in screen record. Mac: Cmd+Shift+5. Windows: Win+Alt+R. |
| 4. Voiceover | 10 min | All 7 VOs in one sitting (options below). One take each. A tiny stumble in your own voice is fine; regenerate TTS only if a word is mangled. |
| 5. Assemble | 30 min | ~4 min per video in CapCut using your saved template (below). Drop clips in order, add text overlays per the script's shot list, drop VO, trim to length. |
| 6. Captions + export | 7 min | Auto captions on every video, quick scan for errors (£ amounts and names are the usual casualties), export all. |
| 7. Schedule | 3 min | Upload to TikTok drafts or schedule via TikTok Studio at the calendar's times. Paste caption and hashtags from the script file. |

If a video is not done after 20 minutes of assembly, simplify it: fewer cuts,
longer holds, one text card instead of three. Then ship it. See the rule at
the bottom.

## CapCut setup (once, ~30 minutes, saves hours forever)

Use CapCut mobile or CapCut web (capcut.com works in a browser, nothing to
install). Build one project called TEMPLATE and duplicate it for every video:

- 9:16 canvas, 1080x1920, 30fps.
- Three saved text presets, matching `brand/brand.json`:
  - Hook card: large text, ink `#221F1C` on a full-screen plaster `#F1E9DE`
    background, centred. Sentence case. One accent word in terracotta `#C4602F`.
  - Overlay label: ink text on a plaster rounded box, bottom third, max two
    lines. Used for the shot-by-shot labels in the scripts.
  - Closing card: plaster background, ink text, brand name plus the CTA line.
- Font: a plain serif (CapCut's built-in serifs are fine; visual match to
  Georgia is close enough, do not chase pixel-perfect).
- No emoji in any on-screen text, ever. Emoji live only in captions, sparingly.
- Safe zones: keep text out of the bottom ~20% (caption bar and buttons) and
  the right edge (like/comment column). CapCut shows overlay guides; trust them.

## Voiceover: TikTok native TTS vs ElevenLabs vs your voice

- TikTok native TTS (free, fastest): add the VO text in the TikTok app,
  apply a text-to-speech voice, then reduce the text layer to near-invisible
  or keep it as captions. Pick one UK-English voice and never change it; the
  voice is the brand's face. Downside: you must finish assembly inside TikTok,
  and the voice is recognisably TTS (audiences do not mind).
- Your own voice (free, warmest): voice notes app, phone near your mouth,
  duvet over your head for a dead room. Read each VO once. You never appear on
  screen, so nerves do not apply. This is the recommended default; the VOs are
  written in short sentences precisely so they read cleanly.
- ElevenLabs (paid, ~£4/month tier): noticeably natural voices. If you use
  it, you need a paid tier for commercial use; check the licence page before
  publishing. Worth it only if native TTS grates and you refuse to use your
  own voice. Do not clone anyone else's voice, obviously.

Whichever you choose, stay consistent. Voice changes reset audience
familiarity.

Note on numbers: before batch one, test how your chosen voice reads "£250"
and "£75-£150". If it stumbles, write amounts out in words in the TTS box
("two hundred and fifty pounds"); the VO scripts already do this.

## B-roll: where to get it legally

Free, royalty-free, no attribution required, commercial use allowed:

- Pexels (pexels.com/videos): best for interiors; search terms are given in
  each script's Visuals section.
- Pixabay (pixabay.com/videos): second pass when Pexels misses.
- Coverr (coverr.co): strong on lifestyle and home clips.
- Mixkit (mixkit.co): good motion/detail shots (steam, light, fabric).

Plus your two home-made sources, which are the most on-brand of all:
- Moodboard screen recordings: record your phone or desktop screen while
  scrolling or building a board. This is unique footage no one else has.
- Canva timelapses: screen record a real moodboard build, then speed it
  8-12x in CapCut. One 20-minute build yields b-roll for several videos.

### Copyright and licence rules (non-negotiable)

- Only the royalty-free sources above, or footage/screens you made yourself.
- No ripped content: no other creators' videos, no TV clips, no Pinterest
  images dropped into videos, no "found" before/afters. If you did not make it
  or download it from a licensed library, it does not go in the edit.
- Music: only from TikTok's own in-app library, added at upload time. Never
  import songs into CapCut from elsewhere; commercial-use rights differ and a
  muted video kills the post. If in doubt, post with VO only; these videos
  work without music.
- Product images in moodboards: fine on screen as part of a genuine moodboard
  walkthrough (that is the actual work product), but never present a
  retailer's photo as your own room.

## Captions and accessibility

- Auto-generate captions in CapCut or TikTok on every video; most viewers
  watch muted.
- Proofread the money lines. Auto-captions mangle "£" amounts reliably.
- Keep the hook text readable for a full 1.5 seconds; it is doing double duty
  as the thumbnail text.

## Export and file hygiene

- Export 1080x1920, 30fps, highest quality CapCut offers.
- Filename = script ID + hook version: `PM-01-h1.mp4`. A re-hooked cut of the
  same video is `PM-01-h2.mp4`. This matches the hooks tracker.
- Keep exports and project files in one cloud folder; re-hooking a winner
  should take 5 minutes, not a rebuild.

## The rule: post, do not agonise

- 20 minutes of assembly per video, maximum. Over budget? Simplify and ship.
- Never delete a post before 72 hours. Early flatness means nothing on TikTok.
- You are not allowed to judge a video by feel. The only judge is
  `hooks-tracker.md` at 72 hours.
- Done and posted beats polished and drafted, every single week. The account
  that posts 14 rough videos learns more than the account that posts 3
  perfect ones, and the tracker turns everything it learns into next week's
  plan.
