# Series 02: money transparency (MT)

Six scripts on money. The rule for this series: every figure is a typical UK
beginner range, said out loud as a range, never a promise. Reference ranges
used throughout (keep these consistent across all content):

- Colour consultation: £75-£150
- Single-room e-design: £150-£400
- Room refresh day: £200-£450

Never say "you will earn". Say "typical", "normal", "a reachable fee".
On-screen numbers in terracotta `#C4602F` on plaster `#F1E9DE`; everything else
in ink `#221F1C`. No emoji on screen.

## MT-04: hourly vs fixed fee for beginners

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Stop charging by the hour

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> Should a beginner charge hourly, or a fixed fee?
> Fixed. Almost every time.
> Here is why.
> Clients hate open meters. A fixed fee lets them relax.
> And you are slow at first. Hourly punishes you for learning.
> A fixed fee rewards you as you get faster.
> So start with a fixed package.
> A single room design, one hundred and fifty to four hundred pounds. Typical UK beginner range.
> Keep hourly only for extras beyond the brief.
> Fixed fee. Clear scope. Everyone breathes.
> The kit's calculator prices both ways. Waitlist in bio.
