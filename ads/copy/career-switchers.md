# Ad copy: career-switchers

**Ad set:** `career-switchers` (£3.50/day, see `ads/meta-plan.md`)
**Angle:** the exit ramp, not the leap. Build the first client while still
employed; quit with proof, not hope. Higher intent than the side-hustler
audience, so the copy is more direct about the cost of staying in the wrong
career, without ever asserting anything about the reader's own job.
**Creative slugs (for `utm_content`):** `career-switchers-v1-exitramp`,
`career-switchers-v2-proof`, `career-switchers-s1-moodboard`.

**Policy notes (applies to every ad below):** no personal-attribute
callouts (nothing shaped like "hate your job?" or "are you unemployed"),
no income guarantees, no before/after earnings claims. The fear of wasting
years is voiced as a general truth about career changes, never as a claim
about the viewer. Fee figures are framed as what designers **typically
charge**, never as promised earnings.

---

## Primary text

### Variant 1, short (~40 words)

Changing careers usually means retraining for years before earning a penny.
Interior design is different: in the UK, residential and e-design work needs
no licence and no degree. The First Client Kit is the 30-day path to proving
it. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 2, medium (~80 words)

The advice "just take the leap" has ruined more career changes than it has
started.

There is a quieter route: build the first paying design client in evenings
and weekends, while the salary still lands. One brief, one invoice, one
piece of proof that the new career is real, before anyone resigns anything.

The First Client Kit covers the part no one teaches: pricing (a typical
first brief runs £150 to £250), a client questionnaire, a plain-English
agreement, and a 30-day plan. £12 at launch. Join the waitlist.

### Variant 3, long (~150 words, storytelling)

Most career changes die in the gap between "I want out" and "I have a plan".

The wanting-out part is easy. The expensive part is the uncertainty: nobody
wants to trade a stable salary for a guess, and nobody should. So years pass
in a career that stopped fitting a long time ago, because the alternative
was a leap with no evidence underneath it.

Here is the evidence-first version. In the UK, residential and e-design work
has no legal gatekeeping: no licence, no required degree. Which means the
first paying client can be built on evenings and weekends, while the current
job pays the bills. A typical first brief goes for £150 to £250. Small money,
but it is proof, and proof changes the decision.

The First Client Kit is the 30-day path to that proof: pricing guide, brief
form, plain-English agreement, client-finding plan. £12 when it opens.

Quit with proof, not hope. Join the waitlist.

## Creative briefs (3)

Faceless throughout: hands, rooms, screens, paper. No presenter, no invented
founder, no fake testimonials or fake numbers on screen.

### Brief 1, faceless video: "Exit ramp" (slug `career-switchers-v1-exitramp`)

- **Format:** 9:16 vertical, 20 to 30 seconds, captions burned in. Sound
  from Meta's licensed library only, something steady, not triumphant.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "exit ramp" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the "career changes do not need a leap" framing).
  Swap in the family's best 3-second hold performer once TikTok analytics
  exist; the ad amplifies the proven hook.
- **First-frame text:** "Career changes fail at the leap. So skip the leap."
- **Beats:** (1) hook over a slow push-in on a desk lamp turning on in a
  dim room, evening implied; (2) overlay "step 1: first client, while still
  employed" over hands laying out a moodboard; (3) overlay "step 2: first
  invoice, typically £150 to £250 a brief" over the kit's real pricing
  page; (4) overlay "step 3: decide with proof, not hope" over a printed
  brief being clipped into a folder; (5) end card, plaster background,
  Georgia serif: "The First Client Kit. Join the waitlist."
- **Voice:** on-screen text only, or calm VO. No urgency reads, no
  countdowns.

### Brief 2, faceless video: "Proof" (slug `career-switchers-v2-proof`)

- **Format:** 9:16, 15 to 20 seconds, text-led, fast cuts.
- **Hook to reuse:** the organic "no gatekeeping" hook family from
  `content/scripts/` (the plain statement of the UK rules), delivered with
  the higher-intent framing this audience responds to.
- **First-frame text:** "Retraining for years is optional. In UK interior
  design, it is not even required."
- **Beats:** (1) the fact over a slow pan across paint chips; (2) overlay
  "what actually stands between here and a first client:" then three cuts,
  one overlay each: "a price list", "a brief form", "an agreement in plain
  English"; (3) kit flat-lay, overlay "ready-made, £12 at launch"; (4) end
  card: "Quit with proof, not hope. Join the waitlist."
- **Note:** keep the rules claim scoped exactly as written (residential and
  e-design, UK); do not widen it to "all design everywhere".

### Brief 3, static image: moodboard flat-lay (slug `career-switchers-s1-moodboard`)

- **Format:** 1:1 and 4:5 exports of the same layout.
- **Concept:** overhead flat-lay on plaster (#F1E9DE): the kit's printed
  30-day plan page centre-frame with a pencil resting on it, fabric swatch
  and sage (#7E8D77) paint chip at the edges. The plan page is the hero,
  because this audience buys the path, not the pretty.
- **Text overlay:** Georgia serif, ink (#221F1C), sentence case, small:
  "Quit with proof, not hope." Terracotta (#C4602F) sub-line: "First
  client in 30 days. Kit £12 at launch."
- **No** faces, no office-misery imagery (no head-in-hands stock shots),
  no money imagery.
