# Series 05: behind the method (BM)

Six build-in-public-lite scripts in the brand's own voice. "We" is the brand,
never a fake founder on camera. These scripts humanise a faceless account,
invite comments, and give the waitlist a reason to exist beyond "sign up".

Hard rules for this series:
- Never invent numbers. BM-06 uses the placeholder [N]; it is posted only when
  the real waitlist count makes the line true. Same for any future milestone.
- Never stage fake "community" or testimonials. Engagement bait (BM-05) asks a
  real question we genuinely act on.
- No emoji in on-screen text, ever. Captions may carry one, sparingly.

## BM-01: the kit we wish existed

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): We are building this in public

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> We are building the starter kit we wish existed.
> Everything you need to land your first paying design client. In one box. In plain English.
> Not a course with forty hours of video you will never finish.
> Templates you fill in.
> A pricing calculator with real UK ranges.
> A thirty day roadmap.
> We will share what goes in it as we build. Including the wrong turns.
> It will cost twelve pounds. On purpose. Starting should not be expensive.
> If you want it first, the waitlist is open in our bio.

**Visuals**

2. 1.5-8s: your own screen recording: a working document or folder of draft templates scrolling (blur anything unfinished you do not want copied). Overlay: "The First Client Kit, in progress".

## BM-04: why most aspiring designers never start

**Hook** (on-screen, first 1.5s): Why most aspiring designers never start

**Length:** 30s

**VO script**
> An honest observation from building this.
> Most aspiring designers do not stall on talent.
> They stall on admin.
> What do I charge?
> What do I actually send a client?
> What do I say when someone asks if I am qualified?
> Each question feels small.
> Together, they form a wall.
> Nobody pushes through that wall on motivation alone.
> They push through with ready-made answers.
> That is the entire reason the kit exists.
> Remove the wall. Keep the talent.
> If that is your wall too, the waitlist is in our bio.

**Visuals**
1. 0.0-1.5s: hook card, "never start" in terracotta.
2. 1.5-7s: stock clip of someone staring at a blank laptop screen (Pexels: "person thinking laptop home"). Overlay: "Not a talent problem".
3. 7-16s: text cards stacking one at a time, building the "wall": "What do I charge?", "What do I send?", "What if they ask about qualifications?". Let them pile up on screen.
4. 16-22s: the stacked cards wipe away in one motion (CapCut swipe transition). Behind them, a clean moodboard. Overlay: "Ready-made answers".
5. 22-26s: text card: "Remove the wall. Keep the talent."
6. 26-30s: closing card: "Waitlist in bio."

**Caption**
The wall is made of small admin questions, not missing talent. We are building the answers. #interiordesign #careerchange #sidehustle #buildinpublic

**CTA:** Spoken close: "The waitlist is in our bio." Closing card: "Waitlist in bio."
